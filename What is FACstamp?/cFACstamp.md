Similar to common corporate law, there is also a cFACstamp that is analogous to corporations.  One or more FACID FACstamp license holders can sign a cFACstamp license agreement.  A cFACstamp provides a buffer in that warranty claims to cFACstamps initially are against the entity that backs the cFACstamp.

However, if the entity backing the cFACstamp does to abide by the warranty claim process, the claim can transfer to the backing FACIDs and FACstamps.

Another fundamental difference between common corporate law and cFACstamps is that warranty claims agains the real FACIDs and FACstamps can be seen through the cFACstamp.  This occurs regardless if the cFACstamp backing entity is a corporation or a government agency.
