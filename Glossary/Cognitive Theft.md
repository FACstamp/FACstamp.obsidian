Cognitive theft, or cognitive bamboozlement, is the theft of thought, when a thief by some means such as emotional manipulation, fallacious reasoning, or misinformation, causes the victim to feel, think, or act in a way counter to how they would otherwise feel, think, or act if they had been exposed to accurate information, unbiased reasoning, and balanced emotional content.

Cognitive theft can be more damaging then financial theft, resulting in harm, pain, and perhaps death.  Cognitive theft is to human cognition is what a computer virus is to a computer.  A computer virus can take control of a computer without the owner knowing.  Being exposed to misinformation, unbiased reasoning, or emotionally activating content can result in the person themselves thinking, deciding, and acting in ways they would not otherwise want or desire, also  without knowing about or conscious of it.

Cognitive theft is the most costly and damaging theft known to mankind.  And as a democracy requires both government leaders and the electorate to operate in a wise and discerning manner, cognitive theft is a key component in the demise of democracies.  Democracies, as is the concept of universal love as described in many [[World Wisdom Traditions]], are inherently non hierarchical - the power rests with everyone, all the people, and no one person or group is inherently special.  Leaders in position of power are subject to the same judicial laws and expectations of virtue and discernment as the electorate if not held to an even higher standard.

There are numerous methods of inflicting cognitive theft:
- false, misleading, or inaccurate information
- [fallacious logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_fallacies) 
- non virtuous [biased reasoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_bias) 
- non virtuous emotional activation (particularly [schadenfreude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schadenfreude))
- [gaslighting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaslighting) 
- [othering](https://www.otheringandbelonging.org/the-problem-of-othering/) and the dehumanization of others
- shifting/[altering the context](https://iep.utm.edu/fallacy/) or subject of the conversation
- altering the meaning of words
